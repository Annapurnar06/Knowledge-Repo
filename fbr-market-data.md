# FBR Market Data: State of Digital Lending 2026

## Overview

This document extracts and organizes all market data, growth rates, NPA benchmarks, competitive signals, and founder theses from the FinBox (FBR) State of Digital Lending 2026 report. Data is structured for use in startup planning, investor decks, and product design.

---

## 1. Macro Lending Landscape

### Overall Market Size

| Metric | Value | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Total retail credit outstanding | Growing rapidly | FY24-25 |
| Secured lending share | Dominant, growing faster | FY24-25 |
| Unsecured lending share | Under regulatory pressure | FY24-25 |
| RBI risk weight increase (unsecured) | 25 percentage points hike (Nov 2023) | Effective FY24 |
| NBFC share of unsecured origination | 74-75% by volume | FY24 |
| Bank share of unsecured origination | 25-26% by volume | FY24 |

### Key Structural Shifts

1. **Secured > Unsecured rotation:** Lenders shifting portfolio mix toward secured products after RBI risk weight increase. LAP and secured MSME growing fastest.
2. **Sachetization continues:** 89.3% of personal loans are now sub-INR 1 lakh. Average ticket sizes declining across segments.
3. **Multi-lending rising:** Borrowers with 3+ active loans rose from 37% to 43.5%. Stacking risk is the #1 systemic concern.
4. **Partnership lending dominant:** 95% of banks use fintech partnerships for digital reach. 87% for critical processes. 55% rate partnerships "very important."

### 2.3 MSME Lending

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| AUM | ~INR 35L Cr |
| CAGR | 13% |

**FBR Insight:** "The MSME credit gap is not a discovery problem -- it's a product design problem. Banks cannot profitably serve INR 5-15K working capital needs with EMI-collateral models. The opportunity is in daily/flexible repayment on micro-tickets."

### 2.7 BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| AUM | INR 1.97-2.78L Cr |
| Growth | 13-14% YoY |
| Gross NPA | 6-8% |
| Micro-BNPL NPA | Up to 9% |
| Key trend | Shift from discretionary to essential-use BNPL |
| AI opportunity | High -- multi-lender visibility, essential-use scoring, stacking prevention |

**FBR Insight:** "BNPL NPAs are a product design problem, not a credit quality problem. When BNPL is used for essential purchases (groceries, medical), repayment rates are structurally higher than discretionary (electronics, fashion)."

### 2.8 EDI (Equated Daily Instalment)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Market size | INR 4,500 Cr/quarter (Paytm alone) |
| Growth | Nascent, explosive |
| Collection cost reduction | 7.5x lower than EMI |
| Risk detection speed | 48-72 hours vs 30-day EMI lag |
| NPA reduction | 20% (Muthoot FinCorp case study) |
| Disbursement growth | 300% (Muthoot FinCorp case study) |
| Key players | Paytm, Muthoot FinCorp, DailyPe |
| Key trend | Moving from merchant-only to broader MSME/agri |

**FBR Insight:** "EDI is the most validated new product category in Indian lending. The data is unambiguous: daily deduction from QR/UPI inflows produces structurally lower NPA, faster risk detection, and dramatically lower collection costs. The challenge is ops -- daily reconciliation at scale requires automation."

**Muthoot FinCorp Case Study (FBR-reported):**
- Launched EDI for small merchants
- 300% increase in disbursements
- 20% reduction in NPA
- 7.5x reduction in collection costs
- Early Warning System (EWS) detects risk in 48-72 hours vs 30 days for EMI

## 3. Partnership Lending & Co-Lending

### Market Structure

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Banks using fintech partnerships | 95% |
| Banks using partnerships for critical processes | 87% |
| Banks rating partnerships "very important" | 55% |
| Co-lending CLA Directions | January 2026 (new regulations) |
| FLDG formalization | February 2026 (new regulations) |
| Embedded credit market (global) | ~$124B |
| Indian consumers using embedded credit | >30% recently |

### Partnership Models

| Model | Description | FBR Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Direct co-lending | Bank + NBFC joint loan | Growing post-CLA Directions; bank gets PSL credit, NBFC gets capital efficiency |
| FLDG-based | Originator provides first-loss guarantee | Formalized by RBI in 2026; enables fintech-to-lender partnerships |
| Embedded lending | Credit at point of commerce | $124B global market; >30% Indian consumer adoption |
| API-based origination | Lender APIs integrated into third-party apps | Dominant model for digital-first lenders |
| White-label | Fintech provides full stack, lender provides capital + license | Growing for fintechs wanting to avoid NBFC license cost |

### Key Regulatory Developments (2025-2026)

| Regulation | Date | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| RBI risk weight increase (unsecured) | Nov 2023 | Shifted capital allocation toward secured lending |
| Co-lending CLA Directions | Jan 2026 | Formalized co-lending structure; enables bank-NBFC partnerships at scale |
| FLDG framework formalization | Feb 2026 | Allows fintechs to provide first-loss guarantees; cap at 5% of loan portfolio |
| Digital lending guidelines enforcement | Ongoing | Mandates KFS, direct disbursement to borrower, regulated entity ownership |
| Proposed fintech repository | Under discussion | Central database of all fintech entities; compliance-native infra becomes essential |

## 7. Founder Theses (FBR-derived)

### Thesis 1: "Cash Flow Object > Loan Object"
Design the product around the borrower's cash flow pattern, not around a fixed EMI. EDI validation: 7.5x lower cost, 20% NPA reduction. Applicable beyond merchants: dairy (cooperative cycles), gig (payout cycles), agriculture (harvest cycles).

### Thesis 2: "Own the Corridor, Not the Capital"
Partner with entities that sit between borrower and income: cooperatives, platforms, QR issuers. 55% of banks rate partnerships "very important" -- they need distribution. Capital is commoditized; distribution to qualified borrowers is scarce.

### Thesis 3: "Compliance is a Product, Not a Cost"
RBI fintech repository + digital lending guidelines + CLA Directions = compliance burden rising. First company to make compliance automated and continuous wins lender trust. Regulatory embedding = structural moat.

### Thesis 4: "The Intelligence Layer is Missing"
India has identity (Aadhaar), consent (AA), data (ULI), rails (UPI). What's missing: the AI layer that turns data into credit decisions, product design, and risk monitoring. Whoever builds this layer owns the value chain.

### Thesis 5: "Voice-First is the Distribution Moat"
55% of new digital finance users from Tier-2/3 in 2024. These users don't download apps or fill forms. Voice AI in local language = only viable access channel for bottom 300M.

### Thesis 6: "Multi-Lending Visibility = Platform Power"
Multi-lending rose from 37% to 43.5%. No lender has visibility into borrower's full exposure. Building this visibility layer makes you indispensable to every lender.
